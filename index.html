About APIGA

The Asia Pacific Internet Governance Academy (APIGA) is a renowned capacity-building program jointly organized by the Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers (ICANN) and the Korea Internet Security Agency (KISA). Its primary goal is to foster awareness and active participation in key Internet governance matters, with a special focus on the Asia Pacific (APAC) region. Building on this global initiative, Local APIGA India 2025 aims to offer undergraduate and graduate students in India an immersive experience to understand Internet governance, ICANN's policies, and the multistakeholder model in depth.

Aligned with the broader objectives of APIGA, the Local APIGA India 2025, spearheaded by prominent organizations in India's Internet governance ecosystem, will be a two-day intensive workshop tailored to address India's unique challenges and opportunities in the digital sphere. This localized program will facilitate discussions, hands-on learning, and capacity building on Internet governance topics relevant to India's rapidly evolving digital landscape. The event will feature keynote presentations, expert-led panels, interactive sessions, and a model ICANN simulation, providing a vibrant environment for participants to learn, engage, and connect with experts and peers in the field.

About ICANN

The Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers (ICANN) is a nonprofit, global, multi-stakeholder organization based in the United States. Its primary role is to manage and coordinate critical technical functions that ensure the secure and stable operation of the Internet. ICANN oversees the administration of key databases related to Internet namespaces and numerical identifiers, working under the framework of the Internet Assigned Numbers Authority (IANA) functions.

ICANN's responsibilities include managing the global Domain Name System (DNS), facilitating the development of policies for DNS internationalization, introducing new generic top-level domains (TLDs), and overseeing the operation of root name servers. It also allocates Internet Protocol (IP) address spaces for both IPv4 and IPv6, delegating address blocks to regional Internet registries, and maintains key registries for Internet Protocol identifiers.

At its core, ICANN is guided by principles that prioritize the operational stability of the Internet, encourage competition, ensure inclusive representation from the global Internet community, and develop policies through collaborative, bottom-up, and consensus-driven approaches.
